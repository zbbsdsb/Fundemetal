# Decomposer — Launch Thread Template

A ready-to-post X/Twitter thread for announcing the Decomposer protocol.

## Thread

**Tweet 1** 🧵
> Every AI-generated plan is a lie.
>
> Not a malicious lie. But a lie nonetheless — a polished module list that mimics understanding without substance.
>
> "User management. Payment system. Recommendation engine."
>
> It looks structured. It's a shell. 🧵👇

**Tweet 2**
> The problem isn't that AI plans are wrong. It's that they *look right*.
>
> You follow the plan. Build module one. Module two. Then you hit a wall that was invisible — a dependency nobody mentioned, an assumption that was false.
>
> The plan didn't warn you. It wasn't looking.

**Tweet 3**
> I built something called Decomposer.
>
> A cognitive protocol that forces the wall into visibility before you walk into it.
>
> Five steps. No AI required. Runs on a whiteboard.

**Tweet 4**
> Step 1: Honesty Fuse 🚨
>
> Before any analysis, state three things:
> • What you can actually do
> • Where you're blind
> • How this most likely dies
>
> If you can't write all three, you're not ready.

**Tweet 5**
> Step 2: Uncertainty Mapping 🗺️
>
> Classify every element: Green (known), Yellow (known unknown), Red (unknown unknown).
>
> Rule: Green must not exceed 70%. If it does, your map is dishonest.

**Tweet 6**
> Step 3: Hierarchical Decomposition 🌳
>
> Not functional decomposition. Cognitive dependency.
>
> Ask: "What must I know before I can know X?"
>
> This reveals the *real* critical path — not the one in your Gantt chart.

**Tweet 7**
> Step 4: Error Budget 📊
>
> For every node: Confidence, Survival Condition, Lethality.
>
> "Alive if: 50 users register in week 1. Dead if: >10% login failures."
>
> No node survives without a testable condition. This is the anti-shell.

**Tweet 8**
> Step 5: Anti-Shell Self-Check 🔍
>
> 5 hard checks:
> 1. Does every node have a survival condition?
> 2. Is there any jargon?
> 3. Is Yellow+Red at least 30%?
> 4. Is there an explicit "I don't know"?
> 5. Can you name the first step in one sentence?
>
> If any fails, fix it. Repeat until all pass.

**Tweet 9**
> The comparison:
>
> Standard AI: "We need a recommendation engine."
> Decomposer: "Recommendation engine — YELLOW. I don't know if collaborative filtering works here. Need 100 users of data to test."
>
> One is a statement. The other is a *testable hypothesis*.

> Decomposer is MIT-licensed. Platform-agnostic. Works with any AI, or with just a whiteboard.
>
> The protocol is free. The honesty is the point.
