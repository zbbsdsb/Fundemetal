# ⚰ Graveyard

> A curated library is defined by what it refuses. This is the record of what we turned away, and why.
> An empty graveyard is proof that no curation is happening. Ours is not empty.

Every entry below was a *plausible* skill — a real idea, seriously considered — that failed the [Curator](skills/curator/SKILL.md) admission gate. The cause of death is stated honestly. Rejection here is not an insult to the idea; it is the price of the roster staying sharp.

---

## On the watch list (not dead, not admitted)

These have a real failure mode but no gate with teeth yet. They are barred until someone designs a *falsifiable* acceptance metric for them. Contributions welcome — bring the gate, not just the idea.

| Candidate | Why it's waiting | What it needs |
|---|---|---|
| **Negative Space** (map what no one in the field is doing) | "No one does X" is hard to verify; the anti-cheat gate can't be checked. | A checkable test distinguishing a load-bearing absence from an untested guess. |
| **Constraint Flip** (interrogate which walls are real) | Overlaps Question Autopsy's constraint-origin phase. | Proof it's more than Autopsy Phase C — or accept the merge (already folded in). |

## How something ends up here

Anyone can propose a skill (open an issue). Every proposal is run through **Curator**. If it's rejected, merged, or folded, it gets an entry above with its cause of death. That's the deal: open contribution, curated admission, transparent refusal.

The skills that made it past this gate are in [`skills/`](skills/). The fact that these didn't is what makes that folder mean something.
