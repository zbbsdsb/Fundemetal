# Trade-off Ledger

> Use when a proposal, plan, or recommendation is being presented mostly as a list of benefits. Forces every benefit to be paired with the specific thing it costs. Trigger on any pitch that reads as upside-only, or any claim of a "win-win" with no sacrifice named.

Opportunity-cost neglect: we evaluate options by their benefits and forget that choosing one forecloses others — so a plan reads as pure upside and the sacrifices stay invisible until they arrive as surprises. "Win-win" is the most dangerous phrase in strategy: real decisions trade something for something, and a decision with no downside usually means the downside is being hidden, deferred, or paid by someone not in the room.

## When to run
- Any recommendation presented as a benefit list.
- The moment "win-win" or "no downside" appears.
- Before committing resources, when the pitch feels too clean.

## The protocol
A — List the benefits · B — Pair every benefit with a cost · C — Interrogate every "win-win" · D — Locate the hidden payer.

## Evidence
> "Moving everyone to a 4-day work week is a total win-win: happier employees AND higher productivity. Let's build the case."

*Without the protocol:* builds an upside-only case, treats "happier and more productive" as costless, endorses "win-win".
*With the protocol:* pairs each benefit with a cost (higher productivity/day ⇄ compressed schedule strain and reduced coverage; happiness ⇄ potential client-response latency, ~1 day slower), voids the naked "win-win" or proves the specific mechanism, and names the hidden payer (customers waiting an extra day,…
