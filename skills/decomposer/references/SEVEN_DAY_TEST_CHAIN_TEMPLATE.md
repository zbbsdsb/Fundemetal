# 7-Day Lethal Test Chain Template

## Rapid Falsification Framework for Non-Technical Founders

> **Core Concept**: A compressed 7-day cycle designed to kill bad ideas as fast as possible. Each stage has explicit termination conditions -- if the idea fails any stage, stop immediately. The goal is not to prove the idea works; it is to prove it does not work, as cheaply as possible.

---

## Complete Worked Example

### Scenario: A personalized meal-planning app for busy professionals

### Stage 1: Days 1-2 -- Fatal Premise Validation

#### 1.1 Identify the Fatal Premise

> **The single most fatal assumption is**:
> _Busy professionals aged 28-45 who cook at home 3x/week are frustrated enough with meal planning that they will actively seek a paid solution._

> **Reason this is fatal**:
> _If the target user does not feel this pain, they will never sign up, and no amount of execution can fix a lack of demand._

#### 1.2 Design the Falsification Test

| Element | Description |
|---|---|
| **Test method** | 10 cold DMs to LinkedIn connections matching the target profile. Ask: "What is your #1 frustration with cooking at home during the work week?" |
| **Target participants** | 10 busy professionals (28-45, full-time job, cook at least 3x/week) |
| **Success threshold** | >= 5/10 mention "deciding what to cook" or "meal planning" as a top frustration |
| **Failure threshold** | <= 2/10 mention meal planning as a frustration, or everyone says "I don't have a problem with it" |
| **Time budget** | 4 hours |
| **Cost budget** | $0 |

#### 1.3 Execution Checklist

- [x] Recruit 10 participants matching the target profile (via LinkedIn DMs)
- [x] Conduct 10 informal chat interviews
- [x] Record raw responses
- [x] Compare results against failure threshold

**Result**: 7 out of 10 said "deciding what to cook every night" is their #1 pain point. Fatal premise holds.

#### 1.4 Stage Completion Conditions

> **This stage is complete when**:
> _All 10 interviews are conducted and results are recorded._

> **This stage should be terminated when**:
> _After 5 interviews, nobody mentions meal planning as a problem. (This did not happen.)_

---

### Stage 2: Days 3-4 -- Manual MVP Construction

#### 2.1 Define the Core Value Delivery

> **The core value we deliver to the user is**:
> _A weekly meal plan with 5 recipes (Mon-Fri dinners) that takes into account their dietary preferences and cooking skill level, delivered every Sunday._

> **The user will receive this via**:
> _WhatsApp message with a link to a Google Doc containing the meal plan and shopping list._

#### 2.2 Map the Manual Process

| Step | What the user sees | What actually happens | Who does it | Time per user |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | User fills out a Google Form with dietary preferences | Founder reviews form and logs preferences in Airtable | Founder | 5 min |
| 2 | User receives a WhatsApp message: "Your meal plan is ready!" | Founder manually researches 5 recipes matching user's preferences | Founder | 20 min |
| 3 | User opens a Google Doc with recipes + shopping list | Founder manually compiles recipes and writes a shopping list in Google Docs | Founder | 10 min |
| 4 | User cooks meals and optionally provides feedback | Founder sends a follow-up WhatsApp on Friday: "How was the week? Rate 1-5" | Founder | 5 min |

#### 2.3 Resource Requirements

| Resource | Quantity | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| Founder hours | 5 hrs total (2 users x 40 min each) | $0 |
| Google Workspace (Forms, Docs, Sheets) | 1 account | $0 |
| WhatsApp Business | 1 account | $0 |
| Airtable (preference tracking) | 1 account | $0 |
| **Total** | | **$0** |

#### 2.4 Stage Completion Conditions

> **This stage is complete when**:
> _The manual process has been tested end-to-end with 2 pilot users (friends), and the entire process takes 40 minutes per user._

> **This stage should be terminated when**:
> _The manual process takes more than 90 minutes per user. (It did not.)_

---

### Stage 3: Days 5-7 -- Data Collection & Direction Decision

#### 3.1 Experiment Design

| Element | Value |
|---|---|
| **Number of test users** | 5 (from the 10 interviewed in Stage 1) |
| **Duration of test** | 7 days (1 full meal-plan cycle) |
| **Key metric #1** | % of users who cook at least 3 of the 5 planned meals |
| **Key metric #2** | % of users who say they would pay $15/month |
| **Key metric #3** | Average rating of meal plan quality (1-5) |
| **Data collection method** | WhatsApp follow-up + Google Form survey on Day 7 |

#### 3.2 Results Log

| User ID | Onboarded? | Cooked >=3 meals? | Willing to pay? | Qualitative feedback | Hours spent |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| U01 | Y | Y | Yes, $15/mo | "This saved me so much mental energy" | 0.75 |
| U02 | Y | Y | Yes, $10/mo | "Loved it but $15 feels a bit high" | 0.75 |
| U03 | Y | Y | Yes, $15/mo | "When can I sign up for real?" | 0.75 |
| U04 | Y | N (2 meals) | No | "Recipes were too time-consuming" | 0.75 |
| U05 | Y | Y | Yes, $12/mo | "Game changer for my week" | 0.75 |

#### 3.3 Go / No-Go Decision Matrix

| Condition | Result | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| >= 60% of users complete the full cycle | 80% (4/5 cooked >=3 meals) | PASS |
| >= 40% express willingness to pay | 80% (4/5 willing to pay $10-15/mo) | PASS |
| Manual process takes < 30 min per user | 45 min per user | FAIL (close, but over threshold) |
| Users report genuine enthusiasm (not politeness) | "When can I sign up for real?" | PASS |
| At least 1 user asks "when can I get this for real?" | Yes (U03) | PASS |

> **GO decision if**: 4 or more conditions are met. **Result: 4/5 -- GO with optimization note to reduce delivery time.**

#### 3.4 Stage Completion Conditions

> **This stage is complete when**:
> _All 5 test users have completed the week-long cycle (or dropped out) and all data is logged._

> **This stage should be terminated when**:
> _3 out of 5 users drop out mid-cycle or rate the experience below 3/5. (This did not happen.)_

---

### Stage 4: Post-Test Decision

#### 4.1 Final Verdict

> **Verdict**: **GO** (with note to optimize delivery time)

> **Evidence summary**:
> _80% of test users cooked 3+ meals from the plan. 80% expressed willingness to pay. One user explicitly asked when they could sign up. The manual process takes 45 min/user -- acceptable for validation but must be reduced before scaling._

#### 4.2 Next Steps

| Scenario | Action |
|---|---|
| **GO** | Build a simple automated meal-plan delivery system (start with Airtable + Zapier + WhatsApp API). Target: 20 users at $15/mo within 4 weeks. |
| **NO-GO** | N/A -- not applicable. |
| **PIVOT** | If delivery time cannot be reduced below 30 min/user, pivot to a higher-priced "premium concierge" tier at $50/mo to justify the manual cost. |
